1. Overview

The FY 2024 PBB Dashboard is a centralized monitoring tool for Partido State University. It tracks documentary submission status, shared remarks, recent updates, and an indicative eligibility self-rating across 18 official PBB requirements.

Important: This dashboard does not upload or store documentary files. Google Drive folders remain the actual submission destinations. The dashboard stores monitoring metadata only—status, remarks, updater name, and timestamps.

The dashboard is organized from top to bottom as follows:

  1. Header with quick links and theme toggle
  2. Central Monitoring Summary
  3. Recent Activity
  4. Search, filters, and refresh controls
  5. Dimensions of Accountability (requirement tiles)
  6. Indicative Self-Rating of FY 2024 PBB Eligibility
  7. Footer with disclaimer and institutional attribution

2. Getting Started

  1. Open the dashboard URL provided by the Institutional Planning and Development Office (IPDO) or the Office of the Vice President for Executive Operations and Institutional Development (Ovpeoid).
  2. Review the Central Monitoring Summary to see overall progress.
  3. Locate your assigned requirement tile under Dimensions of Accountability (Results Areas).
  4. Use Open Submission Folder to access the Google Drive folder for document upload outside this system.
  5. After uploading documents, return to the dashboard, mark the requirement as submitted, and add remarks if needed.
  6. For internal planning, complete the Indicative Self-Rating section at the bottom and click Save Eligibility Assessment.

3. Header Tools

The top header provides quick access to reference materials and display options.

  • View Guidelines — Opens the official FY 2024 PBB Guidelines PDF in a new browser tab.
  • Open Reporting Forms — Opens the shared Google Drive folder containing reporting forms used for PBB submissions.
  • User Manual & FAQs — Opens this guide.
  • Theme toggle (sun/moon icon) — Switches between light and dark display modes. Your preference is saved in the browser.

4. Central Monitoring Summary

This card shows the overall documentary submission progress for all 18 requirements.

  • Completion percentage — Circular indicator and progress bar showing submitted vs. total requirements.
  • Counts — Displays submitted, pending, and total requirement counts.
  • Last synchronized — Timestamp of the most recent data refresh from the database.
  • Connection status — Shows whether the dashboard is online and receiving live updates.

When all 18 requirements are marked submitted, a success message appears in this section.

5. Recent Activity

The Recent Activity panel lists the five most recent dashboard changes, such as submission status updates, remarks saves, and eligibility assessment updates.

  • Each entry shows who made the change, what was changed, and when.
  • Click History to open the complete activity log (up to 100 recent entries).

6. Search & Filters

Use the controls below Recent Activity to find specific requirements quickly.

  • Search — Search by requirement title, description, validating agency, deadline, or keywords.
  • Category filter — Filter by Performance, Process, Financial, Citizen/Client Satisfaction, or Agency Accountability.
  • Status filter — Show all, pending only, or submitted only.
  • Sort — Default is Official order so tiles stay in their prescribed sequence. Other options include title and status.
  • Refresh — Manually reload the latest data from the server.
  • Clear filters — Resets search and filter selections.

Filter preferences are saved in your browser for convenience.

7. Requirement Tiles

Requirements are grouped under Dimensions of Accountability (Results Areas):

  • Performance Results — Four MFO tiles (Higher Education, Advanced Education, Research, TAEP)
  • Process Results, Financial Results, and Citizen/Client Satisfaction Results — Displayed in equal columns
  • Agency Accountabilities — Separate section below the results areas

Each requirement tile includes:

  • Category badge and requirement title
  • Status badge (Pending or Submitted)
  • Description and documentary checklist (expandable)
  • Validating agency and deadline information
  • Open Submission Folder — Opens the Google Drive folder
  • Copy Folder Link — Copies the folder URL to your clipboard
  • Submitted toggle — Marks the requirement as submitted or pending
  • Remarks field with Save Remarks button
  • Submitted date, last updated timestamp, and updater name
  • View Update History — Per-requirement audit trail

Marking a requirement as submitted

  1. Confirm documents are uploaded to the correct Google Drive folder.
  2. Toggle Submitted to ON.
  3. Enter your name or office when prompted, then click Continue.
  4. The monitoring summary updates automatically for all users.
Turning a requirement back to Pending requires confirmation, since this affects the overall completion percentage.

8. Remarks

Each requirement tile has a shared remarks field for submission details, deficiencies, follow-up actions, proof-of-submission notes, or validation status.

  1. Type your remarks in the text area (maximum 1,000 characters).
  2. Click Save Remarks. The button is enabled only when there are unsaved changes.
  3. Enter your name or office when prompted.
  4. Wait for the Saved confirmation before closing the page.
Remarks are visible to all dashboard users. Do not enter confidential, personal, or sensitive information. This disclaimer also appears in the dashboard footer.

9. Indicative Self-Rating of FY 2024 PBB Eligibility

Located at the bottom of the dashboard, this section provides an internal planning tool for estimating PBB eligibility on a 100-point scale. It is not the official Final Eligibility Assessment.

The self-rating covers four result dimensions plus complaint handling:

  • Performance Results — Total applicable indicators and number fully met
  • Process Results — Percentage improvement in the nominated critical service
  • Financial Results — FY 2024 Disbursement BUR percentage
  • Hotline #8888 — Ticket count, resolution rate, or “no complaints”
  • Contact Center ng Bayan — Ticket count, resolution rate, or “no complaints”

Before any inputs are entered, each criterion displays a baseline Rating 1 (the lowest possible score). As you enter values, the score panel on the right updates in real time.

After completing the form, click Save Eligibility Assessment. The score panel shows:

  • Total score out of 100
  • Status (Not Yet Assessed, Indicatively Eligible, or Below Eligibility Threshold)
  • Per-criterion rating and points
  • Isolation/exclusion risk warning when any criterion is rated below 4
  • Estimated indicative PBB rate (% of Monthly Basic Salary), when applicable

10. Reportorial Submission Timeliness

Within the eligibility section, the Reportorial Submission Timeliness area tracks whether agency accountability reports were submitted on time.

  1. Toggle All reportorial requirements were submitted on time OFF if any requirement was late.
  2. For each late item, switch its Late toggle ON. The first two items in the list are the APR timelines:
    • BFAR for the fourth quarter of FY 2024 (January 30, 2025)
    • Submission of Annual APR to DBM Regional Office (February 16, 2025)
  3. Enter the actual submission date and reason/remarks for each late item.
  4. Click Save Eligibility Assessment.

If any reportorial requirement was late, a possible 5% reduction may be applied to the estimated indicative PBB rate shown in the score panel.

11. Collaboration & Synchronization

The dashboard is designed for multi-user, multi-browser collaboration without login credentials.

  • Data refreshes automatically every 5 seconds.
  • All users see the same submission statuses, remarks, and progress.
  • Before any change, you must enter your name or office. This name is stored in your browser and reused for future updates.
  • If two users edit the same item simultaneously, the system uses version checking. The second save may be rejected with a message to reload and review the latest data.

12. Mobile Use

The dashboard is responsive and usable on phones and tablets.

  • Requirement tiles stack vertically on smaller screens.
  • Search, filters, and action buttons expand to full width for easier tapping.
  • The completion circle in the monitoring summary maintains its shape on mobile.
  • Use landscape orientation when reviewing long requirement descriptions or the eligibility form.

13. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Do I need a username and password?
No. The dashboard uses public collaborative editing. Anyone with the URL can view and update monitoring data. You identify yourself by entering your name or office each time you make a change (your browser remembers it for convenience).
Does this dashboard store our uploaded documents?
No. Documents must be uploaded to the designated Google Drive folders linked on each requirement tile. The dashboard only records whether submission is complete, along with remarks and audit metadata.
Why do I need to enter my name or office before saving?
All changes are shared and visible to every dashboard user. Recording the updater name supports accountability and appears in the activity log and requirement history.
My remarks or eligibility inputs disappeared. What happened?
Unsaved text may be overwritten when the dashboard refreshes from the server (every 5 seconds). Always click Save Remarks or Save Eligibility Assessment before leaving the page. If another user saved changes first, reload the page and review the latest data.
Why did my save fail with a conflict message?
Another user updated the same requirement or eligibility record while you were editing. The dashboard loads the latest version automatically. Review the current values and save again if needed.
Does marking a requirement as submitted upload files automatically?
No. You must upload files to the Google Drive folder first, then mark the requirement as submitted on the dashboard to update the monitoring status.
Can I undo marking a requirement as submitted?
Yes. Toggle Submitted back to OFF. You will be asked to confirm because this changes the overall completion percentage for all users.
Is the eligibility self-rating the official DBM result?
No. It is an indicative internal tool for planning and monitoring only. Final eligibility, exclusions, validation results, and applicable PBB rates remain subject to the official Final Eligibility Assessment under the FY 2024 PBB Guidelines.
Why does the score show Rating 1 before I enter anything?
Rating 1 is the lowest possible score for each criterion. The dashboard uses this as a baseline (20.00 / 100.00 total) until you enter actual self-rating inputs. Status remains Not Yet Assessed until meaningful data is provided and saved.
Do I need to mark requirements submitted before using the eligibility section?
No. The eligibility self-rating is independent of requirement submission statuses. You can complete and save it at any time.
What should I put in the remarks field?
Use remarks for submission details, noted deficiencies, follow-up actions, proof-of-submission references, or internal validation notes. Avoid confidential, personal, or sensitive information.
How often does the dashboard update?
The dashboard polls for new data every 5 seconds. You can also click Refresh for an immediate update. The Last synchronized timestamp in the monitoring summary shows when data was last loaded.
Who maintains this dashboard?
The dashboard is maintained for internal monitoring by the Office of the Vice President for Executive Operations and Institutional Development and the Institutional Planning and Development Office, Partido State University.
